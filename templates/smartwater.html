What Are Smart Water Meters?

Smart water meters are IoT-enabled devices that automatically measure and transmit water consumption data in real-time. They eliminate the need for manual meter readings and provide accurate, instant insights into water usage patterns.

Wireless Connectivity

Meters connect via LoRaWAN, NB-IoT, or WiFi to transmit data automatically to our cloud platform.

Real-Time Monitoring

View current water consumption and historical data anytime, anywhere through your dashboard.

Data Analytics

Advanced algorithms detect leaks, anomalies, and provide actionable insights for water conservation.

How Smart Water Meters Work

1
Water Flow Detection

The meter uses ultrasonic or electromagnetic sensors to precisely measure water flow through pipes without any moving parts, ensuring accuracy and longevity.

2
Data Collection & Processing

The built-in microprocessor collects consumption data, timestamps it, and stores readings at regular intervals (typically every 15 minutes to 1 hour).

3
Wireless Transmission

Data is encrypted and transmitted securely via wireless networks (LoRaWAN, NB-IoT, or WiFi) to our cloud servers.

4
Integration with Billing System

Our system automatically receives the meter readings, processes them, and updates tenant accounts in real-time. Bills are generated automatically based on actual consumption.

5
Alerts & Notifications

The system monitors for unusual patterns, leaks, or excessive consumption and sends instant SMS/email alerts to property managers and tenants.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost?

Pricing varies based on property size. Typical cost is $80-120 per meter including installation. We offer flexible payment plans and subscription models. Contact us for a custom quote.

How long does installation take?

For a typical 50-unit property, full installation takes 3-5 days. Each individual meter installation takes 30-45 minutes. We schedule installations to minimize tenant disruption.

What if there's no network coverage?

We offer multiple connectivity options (LoRaWAN, NB-IoT, WiFi). If cellular coverage is poor, we can install a dedicated gateway with internet connectivity to ensure reliable data transmission.

What happens during power outages?

Smart meters have built-in batteries lasting 10+ years and continue recording data during outages. Data is stored locally and transmitted once connectivity is restored.

Can tenants see their usage?

Yes! Tenants can access a mobile app or web portal to view real-time consumption, historical trends, and receive alerts. This transparency promotes water conservation and reduces disputes.

What's the return on investment (ROI)?

Most property managers see ROI within 18-24 months through labor savings, reduced water waste, fewer disputes, and accurate billing. Water loss detection alone can save 15-25% on water costs.
